Scotland’s Championship Golf Courses: A Golfer’s Dream Itinerary

Scotland, often hailed as the "Home of Golf," offers some of the most iconic and scenic golf courses in the world. The country's rich golfing heritage, combined with its diverse landscapes, makes it a golfer’s dream destination. For those looking to embark on an unforgettable golf tour, Scotland boasts championship-level courses that cater to every type of golfer, from beginners to seasoned pros. In this itinerary, we’ll guide you through Scotland’s finest championship courses, offering you an ultimate golfing experience. For more information on golf breaks in Scotland, check out this golf break guide.

Day 1: Arrival in Edinburgh and the Open Championship Experience

Edinburgh: A Gateway to Scottish Golf

Arrive in Edinburgh, a city with rich history, beautiful landscapes, and easy access to some of Scotland’s best golf courses. Known for its medieval charm and cosmopolitan atmosphere, Edinburgh is an ideal starting point for your golfing adventure.

Royal Musselburgh Golf Club

Your first stop should be the historic Royal Musselburgh Golf Club, located just a short drive from Edinburgh. Founded in 1774, it is one of the oldest golf clubs in the world. The course offers a challenge to golfers of all abilities, with tight fairways and well-placed bunkers.

Accommodation Recommendation:

Check in to a local boutique hotel or a luxury golf resort in the heart of Edinburgh, such as The Balmoral Hotel or a charming guesthouse.

Day 2: The Old Course at St Andrews – A Golfer's Pilgrimage

St Andrews: The Birthplace of Golf

No golf itinerary in Scotland would be complete without a pilgrimage to St Andrews, the birthplace of golf. Just a 1.5-hour drive from Edinburgh, this iconic destination is home to the legendary Old Course.

The Old Course at St Andrews

The Old Course is perhaps the most famous golf course in the world. With its wide fairways, towering sand dunes, and challenging bunkers, it’s a must-play for any golf enthusiast. The course has hosted the Open Championship 29 times, and stepping onto its hallowed grounds is a dream come true for golfers. Be sure to play on a day when it is open to the public, or alternatively, participate in a lottery to secure a tee time.

Accommodation Recommendation:

Stay at one of St Andrews’ luxurious hotels like The Old Course Hotel or a cozy bed and breakfast near the course. St Andrews also offers plenty of charming pubs and restaurants to relax after your round.

Day 3: Royal Aberdeen Golf Club – A True Challenge

Royal Aberdeen Golf Club: A Hidden Gem

Just a 90-minute drive from St Andrews is the Royal Aberdeen Golf Club, one of Scotland’s oldest and most respected courses. Located on the northeastern coast of Scotland, Royal Aberdeen offers a traditional links challenge with panoramic views of the North Sea. The course is known for its natural beauty and difficulty, with wind often playing a major role in how golfers approach the holes.

The Balgownie Links

The Balgownie Links at Royal Aberdeen is a true test of golf, with deep bunkers, narrow fairways, and tough greens. This course regularly hosts high-profile events and is considered one of the best courses in the UK.

Accommodation Recommendation:

Stay in Aberdeen, the vibrant city that boasts fine dining, historic architecture, and a variety of entertainment options. Choose a hotel like The Marcliffe Hotel and Spa for a relaxing evening after your round.

Day 4: Cruden Bay Golf Club – The Stunning Coastal Links

Cruden Bay Golf Club: A Scenic Adventure

Your next destination should be Cruden Bay Golf Club, located just 20 miles north of Aberdeen. This course is a true gem, with stunning views of the North Sea and a layout that blends natural beauty with challenging golf. It’s often ranked among Scotland’s top 100 courses and features unique holes that make use of the coastal landscape.

The Beauty of Cruden Bay

Cruden Bay is a traditional links course, offering a fantastic combination of rolling dunes, challenging hazards, and breathtaking coastal scenery. The course is renowned for its difficulty and charm, making it a favorite among serious golfers.

Accommodation Recommendation:

Stay at the Cruden Bay Hotel, an iconic spot offering stunning views of the coastline and easy access to the course.

Day 5: Royal Troon Golf Club – A Legendary Course

Royal Troon Golf Club: A Historical Icon

A 3.5-hour drive south from Cruden Bay will bring you to the famed Royal Troon Golf Club. Located on the southwest coast of Scotland, Troon is home to one of the most prestigious courses in the world. Royal Troon regularly hosts the Open Championship and offers a traditional Scottish links golf experience.

The Old Course at Royal Troon

The Old Course at Royal Troon is one of the most challenging tests of golf, featuring deep bunkers, tall rough, and unpredictable weather. The iconic Postage Stamp hole, a par-3 that is one of the shortest but most challenging holes in golf, is a signature feature of the course.

Accommodation Recommendation:

Troon offers a variety of accommodations, from seaside resorts to luxury hotels. The Marine Hotel, located just a short distance from the course, offers an excellent stay with panoramic views of the Ayrshire coast.

Day 6: Trump Turnberry – A Majestic Coastal Resort

Trump Turnberry: World-Class Golf and Spectacular Views

Located a short drive from Royal Troon, Trump Turnberry offers an unforgettable golfing experience. Set against the backdrop of the stunning Ayrshire coast, Turnberry is home to the Ailsa Course, another frequent host of the Open Championship.

The Ailsa Course at Trump Turnberry

The Ailsa Course is a true championship layout, with its spectacular seaside holes and rolling terrain. The course offers one of the most beautiful settings for golf, with dramatic views of the Atlantic Ocean and the nearby Ailsa Craig island. It’s a demanding yet fair test of golf that promises to leave you in awe.

Accommodation Recommendation:

Trump Turnberry Resort is a luxurious spot for golf lovers, offering world-class amenities, fine dining, and spectacular views of the course.

Day 7: The King’s Course at Gleneagles – A Final Challenge

Gleneagles: Scotland’s Premier Resort

Your final stop on this golf tour takes you to the legendary Gleneagles, a luxury resort located in the Scottish Highlands, just 1.5 hours from Edinburgh. The King’s Course at Gleneagles is one of the finest inland courses in the country, and its beautiful setting amidst the rolling hills makes it a fitting conclusion to your golfing adventure.

The King’s Course at Gleneagles

The King’s Course is a challenging par-70 layout that winds through heather, pine trees, and Scottish countryside. It has hosted numerous prestigious events, including the Ryder Cup in 2014. The course’s combination of beauty and difficulty makes it a perfect finale to your week-long golfing experience in Scotland.

Accommodation Recommendation:

Stay at Gleneagles Hotel, which offers a world-class experience with its luxurious spa, fine dining, and numerous recreational options.

Conclusion: A Golfer's Dream Fulfilled

By the end of this golf tour, you will have played on some of the most historic and renowned courses in the world. From the ancient fairways of St Andrews to the challenging links of Royal Troon, each stop on this itinerary offers a unique golfing experience. Scotland truly is a golfer’s dream, with its rich traditions, stunning landscapes, and championship courses. Whether you’re a seasoned pro or a passionate enthusiast, this itinerary will leave you with memories to last a lifetime.
